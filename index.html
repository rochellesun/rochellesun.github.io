BIO

I am a Senior Analyst at Analysis Group specializing in health economics and outcomes research. My work evaluates the clinical and economic impacts of emerging medical therapies through statistical analysis and evaluative modeling. These research projects have been published in peer-reviewed journals, presented at academic conferences, and relied upon by global regulatory agencies in health technology assessment submissions.

At Analysis Group, I also coordinate healthcare pro bono research efforts on behalf of the firm, managing over 50 ongoing research teams collaborating with multiple global health nonprofits. I am a member of the associate board for the International Alliance for Medical Action (ALIMA).

RESEARCH

At Analysis Group, I have worked on numerous modeling projects to evaluate the impact that novel pharmaceutical interventions are estimated to have on the existing patient population. Select case examples include a cohort Markov cost-effectiveness model comparing long-term clinical and economic benefits of anti-obesity medications and a population model estimating the impact of a novel cell therapy on racial disparities among patients indicated for hematopoietic stem cell transplantation. Other projects include matching-adjusted indirect comparisons among treatments for the same indicated disease and physician chart reviews to inform evaluations of treatment patterns, real-world effectiveness, and burden of illness.

Through multiple pro bono engagements, I have also had the opportunity to work on data analysis for NIH-funded clinical trials in HIV and tuberculosis research, impact modeling for large-scale COVID-19 contact tracing programs, and grant proposal-writing to support continued global health research.

Outside of work, I am interested in understanding the role of healthcare as a soft power tool in international relations. Specifically, I am interested in evaluating the evolving role that healthcare holds in diplomatic relations and conflict. This fall, I will begin pursuing my doctoral studies in political science at Harvard University.

GHESKIO is a Haitian-run medical clinic and research center, specializing in treatment of HIV/AIDS, TB, and other infectious and chronic diseases. The clinic has continued to provide care, free of charge, to over 120,000 patients annually in spite of catastrophic natural disasters and nationwide political turmoil.
